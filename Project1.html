Project 1: 2021 Climathon

What I learned

Before the climathon I had very minimal experience with Tableau. I was introduced to the software in a my club (BDAA) meeting. The problem that we had to solve wasn't easy. The Byrd Polar Research Center gave us a data set with 100,000 rows and about 30 parameters to visualize. It took a bit of time to for me to just upload the data into a workbook. When handling such a large data set, I learned that organization is of the utmost importance. At first, I had my data strewn about in different workbooks. Then, after a bit of headscratching my team and I decided to clean the data and consolidate it into one file. The data was ready to go, but I needed to learn how to implement my vision in Tableau. The internet came in handy. I searched how to create a display data on a background image. The last major difficulty was making the visualization interactive. My teammate and I painstakingly figured out how to make the timeline correlate to the data at all nine stations. There were no internet guides. We just clicked around until we stumbled upon the right button. It wasn't pretty, but we persevered.

Improvements

  • Correlating temperature with color at each station
  • Creating a dashboard showing plots for various time tables (ex. 1 month, 6 months, 1 year)
  • Creating visualizations for all parameters given (wind speed, humidity)
