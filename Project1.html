Project 1: 2021 Climathon

My Experience

Before the 2021 Climathon, I had minimal experience with Tableau. I was introduced to Tableau in my club, OSU's Big Data Analytics Association, meeting a week before the competition. The data the organizers wanted visualized was challenging. It consisted of 100,000 rows and about 30 parameters. I spent a significant amount of time to upload the data into a Tableau workbook. When handling such a large data set, I learned that organization is of the utmost importance. At first, I had my data strewn about in different workbooks. Then, after a bit of headscratching, my team and I decided to clean the data and consolidate it into one file. The data was ready to go, but I needed to learn how to implement my vision in Tableau. The internet came in handy. I searched how to create a display data points on a static background image. The last major difficulty was making the visualization interactive. My teammate and I painstakingly figured out how to make the timeline correlate to the data at all nine stations. There were no internet guides. We just clicked around until we stumbled upon the right button. It wasn't pretty, but we persevered. We created the visualization above which details the temperature at the Polar Research Center's climate sensor stations at various times of the year. The location of the data points correspond to the location of the stations on google maps. My team was awarded the best data visualization prize for our efforts.
